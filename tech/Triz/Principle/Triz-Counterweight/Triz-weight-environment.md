[name::weight-environment]
[SpocWebEntityId::22255]
[TrizPrinciple::[[Triz-Counterweight]]]

To compensate for the weight of an object, make it interact with the environment (e.g. use aerodynamic, hydrodynamic, buoyancy and other forces).

## Examples: 

- #has_/examples :: Hydrofoils lift Ship out of the Water to reduce Drag.
- #has_/examples :: Rear Wing of a Racing Car increases Pressure to the Ground.
- #has_/examples :: Vortex Strips improve Lift of Aircraft Wings.
- #has_/examples :: Aircraft Wing Shape reduces Air Density above the Wing, increases Density below Wing, to create Lift. (This also demonstrates Principle 4, Asymmetry).
